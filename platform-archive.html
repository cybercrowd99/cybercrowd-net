CyberCrowd Platform Archive

CyberCrowd Platform Archive is the proof depository for the CyberCrowd system. It exists to preserve build history, governance records, deployment lessons, rollback events, access rules, WDIG logic, Magic Cursor logic, platform evidence, and operational memory.

Archive Purpose

CyberCrowd is the glass castle. The Platform Archive is part of the mortar. It helps the system remember what happened, why decisions were made, what changed, what failed, what was corrected, and what proof exists.

What the Platform Archive May Hold

  • Build history
  • Deployment records
  • Rollback lessons
  • Incident notes
  • Governance records
  • Ethics and conduct references
  • Access pass decisions
  • WDIG routing notes
  • Magic Cursor decisions
  • Cam package records
  • Viewer upgrade rules
  • Creator control decisions
  • Secure surface hardware notes
  • Public discourse signals
  • Client investigation triggers

Observation Keeps the System Alive

CyberCrowd uses observation as a survival rule. If a deployment fails, a routing decision changes, a governance signal appears, or a platform event creates confusion, the Archive helps preserve what was observed and what was learned.

Deployment Integrity

CyberCrowd must preserve deployment integrity. When multiple deployments exist, the system should inspect manifests, verify asset bundles, identify broken deployments, and only roll back or promote after confirming the correct deployment state.

Access and Governance Proof

Access rules, governance decisions, disclosure events, silence events, transparency triggers, hidden influence reviews, and board procedure history may become archive material when they affect platform trust.

Connection to the CyberCrowd System

Platform Archive may connect to WDIG Routing, Magic Cursor, Live Control Room, Cam Packages, Viewer Upgrades, Creator Control, Public Discourse, Secure Surface Hardware, Access Passes, Atomic Kricket, Reten Records, and future investigation workflows.
