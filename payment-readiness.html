CyberCrowd Payment Readiness

CyberCrowd Payment Readiness defines the preparation layer for future paid access, viewer upgrades, cam packages, event access, replay/VOD, adWorm, subscriptions, and billing systems.

Payment Readiness Purpose

CyberCrowd should not wire payment systems before the access rules are clear. Payment Readiness exists to define what may become billable, what access is unlocked, what is temporary, what is event-based, and what must be auditable.

Possible Paid Areas

  • Viewer upgrades
  • Extra camera angle access
  • Cam packages
  • Trial-to-upgrade paths
  • Event access
  • Replay and VOD access
  • Creator/client packages
  • Access passes
  • WDIG Access Pass licensing
  • adWorm paid publication
  • adWorm Enterprise services
  • Future subscriptions

Access Before Billing

CyberCrowd must define the access model before money moves. A payment should correspond to a clear permission: what is unlocked, for whom, for how long, on which surface, and under what rules.

Billing Questions to Define

  • What is being purchased?
  • Is the access temporary, permanent, event-based, or subscription-based?
  • Which camera angles, replays, rooms, or surfaces are unlocked?
  • Does the purchase belong to a viewer, creator, client, device, or event?
  • What happens if access fails?
  • What proof should be preserved?
  • What recovery or refund review path applies?

Stripe Later

Stripe may later support CyberCrowd payments, subscriptions, upgrades, event purchases, access passes, recovery fees, or adWorm billing. Stripe should only be wired after CyberCrowd defines the access model and product boundaries.

x402 Later

x402 or agent-native payments may later support machine-readable paid access or agent-driven transactions. Those systems should only be added after the human access model, security model, and payment purpose are clear.

Payment Proof

Payment-related events may become proof objects when they affect access, billing disputes, viewer upgrades, client trust, adWorm analytics, event access, recovery, or governance review.

Connection to the CyberCrowd System

Payment Readiness may connect to Viewer Upgrades, Cam Packages, Event Access, Replay and VOD Access, Trial Access, Access Passes, License Recovery, Device Trust, Creator Control, adWorm Rotator, Client Investigation, Platform Archive, WDIG Routing, Magic Cursor, Atomic Kricket, and Reten Records media events.
