CyberCrowd System Map

CyberCrowd System Map explains how the platform pieces connect before they become full production tools. CyberCrowd is not a linear feed, single app, or simple website. It is a rotating, multi-surface platform foundation.

Core System Flow

CyberCrowd begins with discovery, routes assets through WDIG, coordinates control through Magic Cursor, operates live activity through the Live Control Room, manages access through passes and upgrades, and preserves proof through the Platform Archive.

Basic Platform Wiring

  • Asset Discovery identifies creators, clients, talent, content, campaigns, surfaces, and proof objects.
  • WDIG Routing asks where each thing belongs before it is placed into the system.
  • Magic Cursor coordinates movement and control across trusted surfaces.
  • Live Control Room operates multi-surface live events.
  • Cam Packages define available camera views and live broadcast structures.
  • Viewer Upgrades define what viewers can unlock or access.
  • Creator Control defines what approved operators can manage.
  • Access Passes define who can enter, view, operate, or control.
  • adWorm Rotator handles approved rotating publication, analytics, and campaign value paths.
  • Public Discourse and Societal Sentiment observe public signal without letting the crowd govern.
  • Governance, Ethics, Boardroom, Silence Events, and Transparency Mode protect the glass castle.
  • Platform Archive preserves proof, version history, deployment lessons, and platform memory.

Multi-Surface Live System

CyberCrowd can later coordinate multiple live surfaces at once. A phone, monitor, camera, control panel, show screen, Reten Records surface, Atomic Kricket surface, and CyberCrowd control surface may all participate in one live operating environment.

  • Monitor A may carry Atomic Kricket culture, show, and magazine energy.
  • Monitor B may carry CyberCrowd control, discourse, and platform operations.
  • Monitor C may carry Reten Records music, video, stage arts, releases, and catalog material.
  • Phone input may feed camera, control, or mobile participation.
  • Magic Cursor may coordinate across trusted surfaces.
  • WDIG may define where each signal, surface, file, or person belongs.

Reten Records Relationship

Reten Records is the music, video, stage arts, records, release, media, and catalog vessel. CyberCrowd is the sandbox, control layer, public discourse engine, and multi-surface platform foundation.

Reten Records can receive and present music, stage arts, sessions, releases, records, and catalog assets. CyberCrowd can coordinate the live control, access, routing, publication, discourse, and archive proof around those events when intentionally wired.

Atomic Kricket Relationship

Atomic Kricket is the culture-magazine and show layer that can move between CyberCrowd public discourse and Reten Records music, video, stage arts, and media material. It can become a bridge for stories, reactions, oddities, records, proof, and audience discovery.

adWorm Relationship

adWorm Rotator is CyberCrowd’s approved rotating publication system. It may support native client placement, paid publication when a placement leaves the client’s own surface, analytics, producer revenue, CyberCrowd platform revenue, adWorm Enterprise services, and future surface-native advertising.

Archive Relationship

The Platform Archive holds CyberCrowd proof: build history, governance, deployment lessons, access rules, incidents, routing decisions, and platform memory. Reten Records may maintain its own archive for music, media, records, sessions, version history, and creative proof. The archives may reference each other later while keeping independent custody.

Why This Map Exists

This page does not claim all systems are live today. It defines how the pieces are intended to relate so the platform can be built in the correct order, with the correct custody, and without forcing everything into one flat app or linear feed.
