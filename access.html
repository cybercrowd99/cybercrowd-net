Access / Trials / Permissions / Entry

CyberCrowd access starts with permission, trust, and clear entry paths.

This page defines the access foundation before CyberCrowd connects accounts, Stripe products, WDIG Access Pass licensing, live camera rooms, subscriber trials, creator dashboards, or controlled media areas.

Viewer Access

Future subscribers may enter public previews, trials, paid rooms, live camera packages, or controlled viewing paths.

Creator Access

Creators may need room setup, media routing, talent controls, camera permissions, package tools, and broadcast readiness.

Device Access

WDIG and trusted devices may support pairing, host-bound access, local control, recovery, and supported-device rules.

Access levels CyberCrowd may support

Public Preview: open crawler-safe pages, basic platform explanation, landing pages, and discovery content.
Trial Entry: limited-time or limited-feature access for viewers, clients, creators, or camera package testing.
Paid Access: future Stripe-connected subscriptions, camera upgrades, live room entry, CyberShop purchases, and media packages.
WDIG Access Pass: future device/host-bound access using a digital flash-drive key model with controlled recovery rules.
Private / Protected Access: administrative, creator, legal, board, content, unreleased, or RED-card areas that must never be public by accident.
This page does not grant access, collect credentials, process payments, or create accounts. It defines the access map before the system is wired.

Simple rule

CyberCrowd should not connect a person, device, payment, camera, media file, or room until the platform knows what kind of access is being requested and whether that access belongs in a public, trial, paid, trusted-device, or protected path.
