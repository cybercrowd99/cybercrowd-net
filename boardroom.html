Boardroom Layer

CyberCrowd Boardroom

The Boardroom is the governance room for platform decisions, definition-first review, disclosure, public signals, silence events, hidden influence, and platform duty. It connects the ecosystem while keeping authority separated and corrections visible.

Rule on the table. Disclosure first. Hidden influence blocked. Public signal clarifies; it does not govern.

Boardroom Purpose

The Boardroom exists because CyberCrowd is the glass castle. Major decisions require clear definitions, honest disclosure, evidence, procedure, and moral discipline.

Definition-First Governance

Decisions begin by defining the issue, event, signal, conflict, risk, or opportunity before action is taken. Definition prevents ambiguity from controlling the room.

Pre-Vote Disclosure

Before major decisions, participants must disclose relevant conflicts, contacts, schedules, pressures, or conversations that could affect the decision. Honest disclosure protects the process.

Hidden Influence Review

Hidden influence, undisclosed pressure, concealed conflicts, or incomplete disclosure may trigger review. The Boardroom must protect platform decisions from covert control.

Silence Events

Silence cannot stall the Boardroom. Silence blocking required clarity may become a procedural signal and be handled through the Silence Events process.

Public Sentiment Boundary

Public sentiment may clarify, but it does not govern. Public reaction may help the Boardroom understand confusion, concern, urgency, or support, but it does not replace board duty, evidence, authority, or platform obligation.

Transparency Mode

Transparency Mode may be used when silence, ambiguity, hidden pressure, public confusion, or platform risk requires clearer observation and public explanation.

Archive and Proof

Boardroom decisions, disclosures, review triggers, silence events, vote concerns, and governance lessons may be preserved through the Platform Archive when they affect platform trust.
