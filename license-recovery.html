CyberCrowd License Recovery

CyberCrowd License Recovery defines how a WDIG Access Pass, device-bound license, host-bound access, or controlled platform permission may be recovered, reviewed, reissued, restricted, or revoked.

License Recovery Purpose

CyberCrowd licenses should not float without custody. A license must remain connected to its access rules, device or host relationship, support status, recovery history, and revocation discipline.

WDIG Access Pass Model

The WDIG Access Pass may use a digital flash-drive key model. It is intended as a one-time access pass that can be device-bound or host-bound, non-transferable, and valid while the device, host, and software remain supported.

Recovery Before Reissue

A license should not be reissued just because someone asks. CyberCrowd should verify the recovery request, review the original device or host relationship, check recovery history, and decide whether the previous license must be revoked before access is restored.

Possible License Recovery Events

  • Lost device or host
  • Damaged device or host
  • Unsupported device or software
  • Compromised access pass
  • Accidental license loss
  • Verified user recovery request
  • Manual review recovery request
  • Paid recovery after allowed recovery window
  • License revocation after reissue

Limited Recovery Discipline

CyberCrowd may later support a limited verified recovery model, such as one free verified recovery within a defined period, followed by paid recovery or manual review for additional requests. The exact terms should be defined before production enforcement.

Revocation After Reissue

When a license is reissued to a new device or host, the old license should be revoked when appropriate. Reissue without revocation can create duplicate access, unclear custody, and platform risk.

Non-Transferable Access

A WDIG Access Pass or controlled CyberCrowd license should not become a transferable object unless the platform explicitly defines transfer rules. Default recovery should protect the original user and platform, not create an open resale path.

Connection to Device Recovery

Device Recovery handles the trusted device or host side. License Recovery handles the permission and licensing side. Together, they protect access continuity without letting licenses drift away from custody.

Connection to Access Passes

Access Passes define who or what may enter controlled CyberCrowd systems. License Recovery defines what happens when the access permission must be restored, reissued, limited, or revoked.

Archive and Proof

License recovery decisions may become proof objects when they affect access, billing, platform trust, device integrity, client investigation, or governance review. CyberCrowd may preserve important recovery events in the Platform Archive.

Connection to the CyberCrowd System

License Recovery may connect to Access Passes, Device Recovery, Device Trust, WDIG Routing, Magic Cursor, Secure Surface Hardware, Creator Control, Client Investigation, Platform Archive, and future payment or licensing systems.
