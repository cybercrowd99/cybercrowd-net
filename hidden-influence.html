CyberCrowd Hidden Influence

CyberCrowd Hidden Influence defines the review layer for undisclosed pressure, concealed conflicts, covert attempts to alter decisions, and hidden influence that may affect platform governance or trusted decision-making.

Hidden Influence Purpose

CyberCrowd must protect the glass castle from decisions shaped by pressure that is not disclosed. A vote, access decision, governance action, or platform choice can be damaged when influence hides behind silence, omission, or incomplete disclosure.

Possible Hidden Influence Signals

  • Undisclosed pressure from outside parties
  • Concealed conflicts of interest
  • Hidden financial interests
  • Private threats or promises connected to a decision
  • Covert attempts to alter board or platform outcomes
  • Incomplete disclosure before major votes
  • Refusal to answer required disclosure questions
  • Misleading silence during governance review
  • Attempts to use public pressure as hidden authority

Disclosure Before Major Decisions

Before major decisions, CyberCrowd may require relevant participants to disclose schedules, contacts, conflicts, and conversation contents connected to the decision being reviewed. Honest disclosure protects the participant. Deceit, evasion, or incomplete disclosure may trigger review.

For-Cause Review

Hidden influence may trigger For-Cause Review. A review does not automatically mean guilt. It means the platform has identified a serious enough signal to require definition, evidence, disclosure, and procedural handling.

Temporary Empty-Seat Logic

If a hidden influence concern affects a major vote or board decision, CyberCrowd may consider whether the affected seat should be treated as temporarily empty for that decision until review or remedy is complete.

Possible Remedies

  • Disclosure correction
  • Vote delay or vote invalidation review
  • Access restriction
  • Seat review
  • Termination-for-cause review
  • Equity or token remedy review
  • Clawback review
  • Legal remedy review
  • Archive preservation of evidence

Archive and Proof

Hidden influence review should preserve relevant proof: disclosures, refusal records, meeting notes, communications, timing, access logs, decision history, and any evidence needed to define what happened.

Connection to the CyberCrowd System

Hidden Influence may connect to Governance, Ethics & Conduct, Platform Archive, Public Discourse, Client Investigation, Access Passes, Creator Control, Live Control Room, WDIG Routing, Magic Cursor, and future board procedure systems.
