CyberCrowd Device Recovery

CyberCrowd Device Recovery defines how a trusted device, host, kiosk, controller, access pass, or surface may be recovered, reissued, restricted, or revoked when access continuity is needed.

Device Recovery Purpose

CyberCrowd access should not float without custody. If a device is lost, replaced, damaged, compromised, or no longer supported, the recovery process must protect the user, the platform, and the original access rules.

Recovery Before Reissue

A device or access pass should not be reissued automatically without review. Recovery should confirm what was lost, what device or host was previously trusted, whether the request is valid, and whether the old access should be revoked.

Possible Recovery Events

  • Lost device
  • Damaged device
  • Replaced phone or host machine
  • Unsupported device
  • Compromised control surface
  • Expired support window
  • WDIG Access Pass recovery
  • Magic Cursor control device recovery
  • Surface OS device recovery
  • Kiosk or trusted display replacement

WDIG Access Pass Recovery

The WDIG Access Pass may use a digital flash-drive key model: one-time, device-bound or host-bound, non-transferable, and valid while the device and software remain supported.

Recovery should be limited, verified, and auditable. A future model may allow one verified recovery within a defined time window, followed by paid recovery or manual review if additional recovery is needed.

Revocation Rule

When access is reissued to a new device or host, the old access should be revoked when appropriate. Reissue without revocation can create duplicate access, unclear custody, and platform risk.

Recovery Review Signals

  • Original device identity
  • Current access pass status
  • Recovery history
  • Reason for recovery
  • Trusted user or account signal
  • Device trust status
  • Surface pairing history
  • Revocation requirement
  • Archive or investigation flag

Connection to Device Trust

Device Trust decides whether a device can participate. Device Recovery handles what happens when that trusted device must be replaced, restricted, reissued, or revoked.

Connection to Access Passes

Access Passes define who or what may enter controlled CyberCrowd systems. Device Recovery protects access continuity without allowing access to become uncontrolled or transferable.

Connection to Platform Archive

Recovery and revocation events may become proof objects when they affect access, licensing, client trust, device integrity, platform security, or governance review.

Connection to the CyberCrowd System

Device Recovery may connect to Device Trust, Access Passes, Surface Pairing, Surface OS, Secure Surface Hardware, WDIG Routing, Magic Cursor, Live Control Room, Creator Control, Client Investigation, Platform Archive, and future licensing systems.
