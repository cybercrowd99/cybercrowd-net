# CyberCrowd / RetenRecords Web Foundation

This repository supports development for CyberCrowd.net and RetenRecords. The official public platform home is CyberCrowd.net.

## Overview

CyberCrowd Net is a discovery-first live media, camera-access, advertising, accessibility, safety, creator-income, viewer-participation, public-discourse, monetary, and digital platform ecosystem.

CyberCrowd is not a basic website, simple storefront, or single-purpose media page. It is being developed as a larger living platform environment where viewers, subscribers, creators, clients, advertisers, accessibility users, families, guardians, communities, public reviewers, and device-based tools can connect through live media access, WDIG control, MultiCam trials, CyberShop products, adWorm exposure systems, Magic Cursor interaction, Reten Records media, CyberCourt public discourse, monetary access systems, and future CRWD platform currency systems.

The long-term goal is to create a platform where creators can monetize their work, viewers can participate in platform value, clients can purchase camera/broadcast access, advertisers can gain exposure through coordinated ad systems, public participants can engage in transparent review systems, and accessibility-focused tools can help users interact with digital spaces more safely and independently.

CyberCrowd is designed to become a coordinated platform for live culture, discovery, media, access, safety, advertising, income independence, public transparency, civic-style discourse, monetary participation, and user-controlled digital experience.

## Purpose

CyberCrowd Net aims to connect audiences with engaging content and facilitate interactions around live cultural, entertainment, creator, client-driven, music, news, archive, public-discourse, and community experiences.

The platform is designed to help users discover, participate in, and benefit from live experiences through camera-access tools, WDIG systems, MultiCam trials, CyberShop products, ad exposure systems, accessibility features, archives, Reten Records media, CyberCourt public discourse, monetary system pathways, and future platform currency systems.

A central purpose of CyberCrowd is to support income independence.

For creators, CyberCrowd is intended to provide tools for exposure, monetization, camera packages, broadcast participation, audience access, ad-supported visibility, Reten Records media publishing, and future platform revenue opportunities.

For viewers, CyberCrowd is intended to move beyond passive watching by creating paths for participation, discovery, rewards, access-based interaction, possible future CRWD participation value, and income-connected opportunities as the platform expands.

For clients, CyberCrowd is intended to support camera packages, live-event participation, broadcast setups, promotional exposure, and hosted digital experiences.

For advertisers and sponsors, CyberCrowd is intended to support exposure through adWorm-style perimeter advertising, page-based promotional space, live-event placements, sponsor visibility, CyberCourt engagement sponsorship, and future coordinated digital ad experiences.

For accessibility users, CyberCrowd is intended to support future tools such as Blind Slate, Deaf Companion, de-escalation support, Magic Cursor interaction, and device-based assistance systems.

For guardians and families, CyberCrowd may later support kid-safe, guardian-controlled access lanes with restricted discovery, approved content, spending locks, privacy protections, and child-safe participation boundaries.

For public reviewers and community participants, CyberCrowd may support future public discourse, survey response, follow-up questions, repair paths, transparency review, and adWorm-sponsored engagement through CyberCourt.

CyberCrowd’s mission is to build a media platform where live culture, technology, accessibility, advertising, safety, public transparency, monetization, and monetary participation work together so creators, viewers, clients, sponsors, guardians, public participants, and users with accessibility needs can gain more independence, opportunity, and value from digital participation.

## Three-Sided Value Circulation

CyberCrowd systems should answer three value questions:

**Me** — how does CyberCrowd survive, grow, pay infrastructure, and remain independent?

**Them** — how does the participant, creator, reviewer, viewer, guardian, advertiser, sponsor, client, or partner receive value or compensation?

**They** — how does the public realm, platform ecosystem, or broader community receive value?

CyberCrowd should avoid dead money, fake participation, unpaid public labor disguised as engagement, and pay-to-control systems.

The goal is structured value circulation with boundaries:

**Me, Them, and They.**

Money, attention, labor, access, and trust should not flow in only one direction.

## Platform Layers

CyberCrowd is being built around several connected layers.

### CyberCrowd.net

Main public platform, discovery pages, CyberShop, WDIG access information, search-indexed public entry point, and future platform portal.

### WDIG

Device-based host/control layer for future pairing, access, camera switching, local control, Magic Cursor, Surface OS, kiosk logic, and platform tools.

### CyberShop

Access point for CyberCrowd products, including the WDIG Access Pass, future MultiCam trial access, platform access products, and future CyberCrowd/Reten Records items.

### Monetary System

Future platform monetary layer for access products, creator income, viewer participation value, sponsor-funded engagement, adWorm campaigns, CyberCourt review pools, Reten Records media sales, Stripe checkout paths, and future CRWD value systems.

### MultiCam

Temporary or subscription-based expanded camera access for viewers, creators, clients, shows, broadcasts, and events.

### adWorm

Advertising exposure layer for perimeter ads, sponsor placement, page-based promotion, creator/client exposure, event visibility, CyberCourt sponsored review pools, and future monetization systems.

### CyberCourt

Future public discourse, docket, survey, follow-up question, repair, transparency, and public sentiment layer.

CyberCourt is not a legal court, customer-service ticket system, or private complaint inbox. It is a public-facing realm for discourse, survey response, follow-up questions, public sentiment, repair paths, transparency, and community engagement.

Core principle:

**CyberCourt gets the questions. CyberCrowd keeps the keys.**

### Reten Records

Future music/media home for audio, teasers, covers, videos, playlists, artist media, music videos, and release media.

### Magic Cursor

Future interaction/control layer for page navigation, live media control, accessibility interaction, chat control, device pairing, and coordinated user experiences.

### Blind Slate

Future accessibility concept for blind and low-vision users, designed around simplified interaction, audio-assisted access, tactile/digital flow, and user-supportive navigation.

### Deaf Companion

Future accessibility concept for deaf and hard-of-hearing users, designed around visual alerts, captions, interaction prompts, safety signals, and coordinated support features.

### De-escalation Tools

Future safety/accessibility layer intended to support disabled users, caregivers, staff, families, or public-facing environments with calmer interaction flows, assistance prompts, and conflict-reduction support.

### Guardian-Controlled Access

Future child-safe and family-safe access layer where guardian-controlled profiles may access approved lanes without adult discovery, open messaging, unrestricted search, or unsafe monetization.

### Identity Step-Up

Future verification layer where viewers may use low-friction access, but creators, owners, broadcasters, payout recipients, channel operators, and room controllers may require stronger identity verification.

### Ad Agent Access

Future declared advertising bot, sponsor agent, and campaign system lane. Advertising agents may buy controlled ad lanes, but may not impersonate viewers, scrape protected rooms, spam creators, or become the crowd.

### Access Records

Future tamper-resistant access record layer based on the principle that CyberCrowd should not rely on silent deletion. Records may be hidden, archived, revoked, quarantined, redacted, or legally removed where required, but platform history should remain accountable.

### Theft Locator

Future locator/safety concept intended for tracking, identifying, or recovering protected devices, assets, access tools, or platform-linked hardware.

### CRWD

Future CyberCrowd internal token/currency concept for platform value, participation, access, rewards, or internal ecosystem transactions.

### Atomic Kricket

Future truth/news platform concept for verified media, news discovery, public-interest content, commentary, and platform-supported information access.

### Surface OS

Future device/surface interaction system for kiosk-style control, paired devices, hosted access, Magic Cursor interaction, and local platform environments.

## CyberCourt Public Discourse Layer

CyberCrowd includes a planned public discourse and repair layer called **CyberCourt**.

CyberCourt is not a legal court, customer-service ticket system, or private complaint inbox. It is a public-facing realm for discourse, survey response, follow-up questions, public sentiment, repair paths, transparency, and community engagement.

CyberCourt is designed around a clear boundary:

**CyberCourt gets the questions. CyberCrowd keeps the keys.**

The public realm may support:

- Public docket visibility
- Survey participation
- Follow-up questions
- Repair discourse
- Community sentiment signals
- Public transparency
- Sponsored civic engagement
- Review participation
- Clarifying questions
- Public confusion tracking
- Public discourse around platform decisions

CyberCrowd operations remain responsible for:

- Safety enforcement
- Child-protection settings
- Creator verification
- Account restrictions
- Payment rules
- Advertiser and bot access
- Device trust
- Legal and security decisions
- Final platform action

CyberCourt may later connect with **adWorm-sponsored review pools**, where participants can be compensated for useful engagement, surveys, clarity signals, and public review.

Compensation does not purchase verdicts, bans, reinstatements, or operational control.

Core principle:

**adWorm funds the room, not the verdict.**

## CyberCourt and the Frivolous Complaint Rule

CyberCrowd may later support a public repair and discourse process for complaints, disputes, bans, and access restrictions.

CyberCrowd should protect honest reports of harm, abuse, fraud, exploitation, child-safety risk, identity misuse, payment fraud, or security threats.

CyberCrowd may also restrict or ban frivolous complaints, false reports, harassment complaints, weaponized accusations, repeated bad-faith escalations, and rule-bypass demands.

A restricted user may be routed into CyberCourt, where the public realm can ask questions, answer surveys, participate in repair discourse, and help clarify whether repair is possible.

CyberCourt does not automatically override CyberCrowd operations.

Core principle:

**CyberCrowd protects honest reporting and blocks weaponized complaining.**

## No Silent Deletion / Durable Record Principle

CyberCrowd should not rely on silent deletion as a trust mechanism.

Access events, identity step-ups, bot declarations, advertiser requests, guardian approvals, creator permissions, media submissions, complaint actions, restriction events, monetary events, campaign approvals, and public docket actions should leave durable records.

Records may later be:

- Hidden
- Ignored
- Archived
- Disabled
- Revoked
- Blocked
- Quarantined
- Redacted
- Placed under legal hold
- Legally removed where required

CyberCrowd should preserve accountability without unnecessarily exposing private, sensitive, financial, or child-identifying information.

Core principle:

**CyberCrowd can hide access. CyberCrowd can revoke access. CyberCrowd can archive access. CyberCrowd can redact what the law requires. But CyberCrowd should not allow silent erasure of platform history.**

Core principle:

## Identity Step-Up for Creators and Owners

CyberCrowd may allow viewers to browse, subscribe, purchase access, watch permitted content, and participate through lower-friction access.

However, a viewer who wants to become a creator, owner, broadcaster, room operator, channel controller, payout recipient, or seller should step up into stronger verification.

Future creator identity step-up may include:

- Government photo ID verification
- Real-person or business verification
- Payment or payout verification
- Legal name and date-of-birth checks where required
- Content ownership acknowledgment
- Platform rules acceptance
- Creator safety rules
- Room and camera control rules

CyberCrowd should avoid storing raw identity documents unless absolutely required. Where possible, CyberCrowd should store verification status rather than raw ID files.

Core principle:

**Watching can be low-friction. Creating, selling, broadcasting, owning, or controlling rooms requires real-person verification.**

## Ad Agent Access

CyberCrowd may later allow declared advertising agents, sponsor bots, or campaign systems to purchase limited advertising placements.

Advertising agents are not treated as viewers, creators, or platform owners.

They may only access approved advertising lanes, submit approved campaign material, operate under rate limits, and pay for clearly defined placements.

Future ad agent access may include:

- Sponsor declaration
- Business or campaign verification
- Payment method verification
- Spending limits
- Rate limits
- Campaign boundaries
- No scraping
- No creator impersonation
- No viewer impersonation
- No protected room access
- No child-profile targeting
- Revocable campaign access

Core principle:

**A bot can buy an ad lane. A bot cannot become the crowd.**
