Broadcast flow foundation

1. Room exists: a creator, client, venue, event, or platform package has a controlled broadcast space.
2. Cameras are assigned: camera positions, device trust, room permissions, and available angles are defined.
3. Viewers enter: public, trial, paid, subscriber, or protected access decides what the viewer can see.
4. Switching is controlled: camera-angle changes, room movement, upgrades, and future package rules are handled by access logic.
5. Media is preserved: teasers, highlights, VOD, Reten Records media, or event archives may route into future media paths.
This page does not stream video, collect viewer data, process payments, or connect hardware. It defines the broadcast map before live systems are wired.

Simple rule

A CyberCrowd broadcast should know the room, the camera, the viewer access level, the device trust state, and the media destination before it becomes a live product.
