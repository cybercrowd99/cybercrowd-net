CyberCrowd Surface OS

CyberCrowd Surface OS is the trusted operating surface concept for kiosks, displays, paired devices, control screens, Magic Cursor coordination, WDIG routing, and multi-surface live environments.

Surface OS Purpose

CyberCrowd is not just a website on a screen. Surface OS gives CyberCrowd a way to think about controlled surfaces: where they belong, what they can show, what they can control, and how they participate in live events.

Possible Surface Types

  • Kiosk surfaces
  • Trusted displays
  • Control monitors
  • Phone-paired surfaces
  • Venue screens
  • Creator control panels
  • Viewer access screens
  • Magic Cursor control surfaces
  • Reten Records media surfaces
  • Atomic Kricket show surfaces
  • CyberCrowd public discourse surfaces

Device Pairing

Surface OS may support future device pairing flows where a phone, monitor, kiosk, camera, or control device is recognized and placed into the correct CyberCrowd surface role.

Connection to WDIG

WDIG asks where a surface belongs. Surface OS gives CyberCrowd a way to define the role of that surface before it becomes part of a live room, control path, archive path, or viewer experience.

Connection to Magic Cursor

Magic Cursor can move across trusted surfaces only when those surfaces are defined. Surface OS supports the map of what Magic Cursor may control, select, route, or coordinate.

Connection to Secure Surface Hardware

Secure Surface Hardware defines device trust and tamper awareness. Surface OS defines how trusted surfaces participate in the CyberCrowd operating environment.

Connection to the CyberCrowd System

Surface OS may connect to WDIG Routing, Magic Cursor, Secure Surface Hardware, Live Control Room, Cam Packages, Viewer Upgrades, Creator Control, Access Passes, Atomic Kricket, Reten Records, and platform archive proof.
