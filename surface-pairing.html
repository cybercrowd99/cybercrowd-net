CyberCrowd Surface Pairing

CyberCrowd Surface Pairing defines how phones, monitors, cameras, kiosks, trusted devices, control panels, and live surfaces connect before they become part of the CyberCrowd multi-surface system.

Surface Pairing Purpose

CyberCrowd should not allow random screens or devices to control a live environment. A surface should be identified, paired, assigned a role, and routed before it participates in camera packages, Magic Cursor control, viewer access, creator control, or archive proof.

Possible Pairing Types

  • Phone to monitor pairing
  • Phone to camera package pairing
  • Phone to Magic Cursor pairing
  • Camera to live room pairing
  • Kiosk to venue pairing
  • Control surface to operator pairing
  • Viewer surface to access pass pairing
  • Creator surface to creator control pairing
  • Reten Records surface pairing
  • Atomic Kricket surface pairing
  • Archive review surface pairing

Pairing Before Control

Pairing should happen before control. A phone, monitor, camera, or kiosk should not become a control surface until CyberCrowd knows what it is, where it belongs, who is using it, and what it is allowed to do.

Connection to WDIG

WDIG asks where the surface belongs. Surface Pairing confirms the device or screen relationship before WDIG routes it into the correct lane.

Connection to Magic Cursor

Magic Cursor may move across paired surfaces. Pairing helps define which surfaces Magic Cursor may point to, select, control, or coordinate.

Connection to Surface OS

Surface OS defines the operating role of a trusted screen or device. Surface Pairing is the connection step that helps that surface join the CyberCrowd environment.

Connection to Secure Surface Hardware

Secure Surface Hardware may provide device trust, tamper awareness, and approval signals. Pairing should respect trust boundaries before a device enters a live control path.

Archive and Proof

Pairing events may become proof objects when they affect access, control, live room trust, device integrity, or platform review. CyberCrowd may later preserve important pairing events in the Platform Archive.

Connection to the CyberCrowd System

Surface Pairing may connect to Surface Roles, Surface OS, Secure Surface Hardware, WDIG Routing, Magic Cursor, Live Control Room, Cam Packages, Viewer Upgrades, Creator Control, Access Passes, Atomic Kricket, Reten Records, and Platform Archive.
