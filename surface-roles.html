CyberCrowd Surface Roles

CyberCrowd Surface Roles defines what each screen, device, input, or control point is supposed to do inside the multi-surface live system. A surface should have a role before it is trusted, controlled, broadcast, archived, or placed into rotation.

Surface Role Purpose

CyberCrowd is not one screen pretending to be a platform. It uses multiple surfaces: phones, monitors, cameras, control panels, viewer windows, creator rooms, public discourse areas, media vessels, show layers, and archive paths. Each surface needs a defined role.

Possible Surface Roles

  • Phone input surface
  • Camera input surface
  • Creator control surface
  • Client control surface
  • Viewer access surface
  • Viewer upgrade surface
  • Live Control Room surface
  • Atomic Kricket show surface
  • Reten Records media surface
  • CyberCrowd public discourse surface
  • adWorm placement surface
  • Archive proof surface
  • Boardroom or governance surface
  • Secure hardware or kiosk surface

Example Multi-Monitor Layout

A future CyberCrowd setup may use multiple monitors and inputs at once. Monitor A may carry Atomic Kricket. Monitor B may carry CyberCrowd control, public discourse, or governance. Monitor C may carry Reten Records media, music, video, stage arts, or catalog material. A phone may provide input, camera control, or mobile participation.

Phone Surface

A phone may act as a camera, controller, authentication point, Magic Cursor companion, live input, creator tool, viewer tool, or paired device surface. WDIG should define where the phone belongs before it controls the system.

Monitor Surface

A monitor may act as a show surface, control surface, viewer surface, Reten media surface, Atomic Kricket surface, archive review surface, or public discourse surface.

Camera Surface

A camera surface may belong to a cam package, live room, viewer upgrade, creator control panel, or archive proof path. Camera surfaces should be identified and placed before viewers are given access.

Trusted Surface Rule

A surface should not gain control just because it appears. CyberCrowd should define the surface role, confirm trust when needed, and route it through WDIG, Magic Cursor, Secure Surface Hardware, or Surface OS before it becomes part of a live control path.

Connection to the CyberCrowd System

Surface Roles may connect to WDIG Routing, Magic Cursor, Surface OS, Secure Surface Hardware, Live Control Room, Cam Packages, Viewer Upgrades, Creator Control, adWorm Rotator, Atomic Kricket, Reten Records, Public Discourse, Boardroom, and Platform Archive.
