Creator / Client / Talent / Content Intake

CyberCrowd starts by identifying what is entering the system.

This intake foundation separates creators, clients, talent, content, media assets, device access, and future broadcast packages before they are connected to payments, licensing, live rooms, or public discovery.

Creators

People or teams preparing broadcast rooms, packages, media drops, shows, or future live camera experiences.

Clients

Brands, venues, organizations, or partners that may need managed broadcast access, event coverage, or platform services.

Talent

Performers, hosts, artists, camera operators, commentators, and support roles connected to CyberCrowd experiences.

Content

Audio, video, teasers, cover art, metadata, room assets, product visuals, and future Reten Records media paths.

What this page does right now

This page is a safe repo addition. It defines the intake categories without collecting private information, accepting uploads, charging money, or creating accounts.

1. Classify Decide whether the entry belongs to creator, client, talent, content, device, media, or shop flow.
2. Route Send the item toward the correct future CyberCrowd system area before attaching payment or access rules.
3. Protect Keep private files, unreleased material, credentials, and RED-card items out of the public web foundation.
This is not the final intake engine. It is the visible platform foundation for future onboarding, asset discovery, content routing, and controlled access.

Future intake paths

CyberCrowd may later connect this foundation to creator applications, client service requests, talent profiles, Reten Records media submissions, WDIG Access Pass checks, Stripe products, camera packages, trial access, and live discovery systems.

For now, this page keeps the architecture understandable while the repo grows one stable piece at a time.
