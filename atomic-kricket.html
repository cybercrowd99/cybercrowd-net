Atomic Kricket

Atomic Kricket is a CyberCrowd show and content engine. It is built for culture, memory, media proof, physical records, oddities, interviews, live sessions, audience discovery, and rotating attention.

CyberCrowd Asset

Atomic Kricket belongs to CyberCrowd as a show engine and media asset. Reten Records may later reference, host, release, preserve, or catalog Atomic Kricket material when appropriate, but the originating asset is CyberCrowd.

Show Engine Purpose

Atomic Kricket is not a linear feed. It is a rotating media engine where the center can shift between a record, a memory conflict, a performance, a strange cultural claim, an archive object, a live session, a casting source, or an audience-discovered story.

Future Segment Types

  • Music memory conflicts
  • Physical record and LP proof checks
  • Archive object reviews
  • Oddity and freak-show performer features
  • Artist and performer interviews
  • Live session reactions
  • Culture and media questions
  • Audience-submitted stories
  • Physical media evidence segments
  • CyberCrowd sandbox experiments

Relationship to Reten Records

CyberCrowd is the sandbox, incubator, and testing world. RetenRecords.com is the public music, records, media, release, show, and catalog vessel. Atomic Kricket may later appear in Reten Records through releases, media catalog entries, live sessions, archive references, or Record Room products.

Rotating Platform Logic

Atomic Kricket supports CyberCrowd’s rotating platform model. It is not built like MySpace, Facebook, YouTube, X, Rumble, or Telegram as a simple linear feed. It is designed to rotate attention between people, proof, performance, culture, records, shows, and discovery.
