CyberCrowd WDIG Routing

CyberCrowd WDIG Routing is the placement layer for the platform. WDIG asks where something belongs before it is moved, surfaced, archived, controlled, broadcast, or connected.

Where We Belong

Where We Belong is not just a slogan. It is CyberCrowd’s routing question. A person, file, device, camera, signal, surface, record, proof object, or live input must be identified, defined, and routed to the correct place.

WDIG Purpose

WDIG helps CyberCrowd prevent confusion by asking where things go before they are placed into the system. The goal is not only movement. The goal is correct custody, correct surface, correct role, and correct proof path.

What WDIG May Route

  • Files and media assets
  • Camera inputs and camera angles
  • Phone inputs
  • Trusted device surfaces
  • Magic Cursor control paths
  • Live session signals
  • Viewer access states
  • Creator and client controls
  • Archive proof objects
  • Record and media references
  • Atomic Kricket show surfaces
  • Reten Records media surfaces
  • CyberCrowd public discourse surfaces

Placement Before Production

CyberCrowd should not throw every object into one feed, one bucket, or one app surface. WDIG supports placement before production, so files, signals, people, and proof objects enter the correct lane.

Connection to Magic Cursor

WDIG defines where something belongs. Magic Cursor helps control and move through those placements across surfaces. Together, they support the multi-surface live system.

Connection to the Archive

When something becomes evidence, history, or a platform lesson, WDIG can route it toward the CyberCrowd Archive. The Archive preserves meaning, custody, proof, and version history.

Connection to the CyberCrowd System

WDIG Routing may connect to Cam Packages, Viewer Upgrades, Live Control Room, Magic Cursor, Secure Surface Hardware, Atomic Kricket, Reten Records, access passes, and platform archive proof.
